## What this file is

The **why** layer behind `voice.md`. Diagnostic, not production: load it when
reconsidering the voice itself, never when writing a page. Writing a page needs
`voice.md` and `products.md`; this file just crowds the context.

### Status tags

- `[RATIFIED]` — confirmed deliberate by the party who can confirm it (the
  practice). Nothing here is ratified yet.
- `[PROPOSED]` — an analytical read awaiting confirmation. **Every `[INFERRED]`
  voice claim lives here and stays here.** The project lead's gut-check at the
  Task 7 checkpoint tests plausibility and presentation only; it does not
  promote anything to `[RATIFIED]`. Only the practice can, and only by saying so.
- `[PREFERENCE]` — taste, honestly labelled, with no strategic claim behind it.
- `[CONSTRUCTION]` — emerged from building the deliverable, no strategic intent.

## Why extraction, not invention

This is a redesign pitch for a practice that already writes in a recognizable
way — plainly, by name, price-first, anxiety-aware. The pitch wins on the
argument that the redesign returns the practice's own voice at a fidelity the
Wix template obscures. So the voice may not be invented to sound "professional."
Every rule traces to something the practice already wrote, or is honestly
flagged as a guardrail the practice has not stated. The two places the copy
genuinely needs *fixing* (the first-person-singular page; the typos) are treated
as errors to correct, never as evidence of the voice.

## Key choices

- **The three personality words are "Named / Plain-spoken / Steadying."**
  `[PROPOSED]` — chosen because each is independently attested in *both* the
  practice's copy and its patients' reviews (a word only the practice used, or
  only patients used, was not trusted). Rejected candidates below.

- **Pricing transparency is treated as a voice driver, not just a fact.**
  `[PROPOSED]` — The practice publishes real prices, which is unusual for a
  dental office and is a genuine differentiator (tension 2 in the brief). The
  *fact* that prices are published is `[OBSERVED]`; the *inference* is that it
  reveals how they talk to patients — as people entitled to information and to
  deciding for themselves, not as leads to be captured with a "call for a
  quote." That inference feeds the "Plain-spoken" personality word and Rules 2,
  4, and 8. If the practice says the published prices are just a Wix habit and
  they'd rather hide them, the personality read weakens — hence `[PROPOSED]`.

- **Rewrite-vs-never-change line (Rule 10).** `[CONSTRUCTION]` — This is the
  project's own contract, not something extracted from the practice, so it is a
  construction, not an inference about them. It resolves tension 1 in the brief.
  The line drawn: **rewrite *how copy reads* (phrasing, grammar, structure,
  first-person→"we", the site's real typos); never change *what copy asserts*
  (any fact, price, name, credential, or patient quote).** Rewritten prose is no
  longer verbatim-citable, and that is *fine* — the never-improvise rule governs
  *facts*, and a fact never comes from rewritten prose; it comes from
  `products.md`. Patient quotes are the one span of "copy" that is also a fact:
  they keep their original typos verbatim (products.md gotcha 12) and may not be
  smoothed. Later tasks lean on this rule hard; it is stated in both `voice.md`
  (Rule 10) and the handoff's survive-if-nothing-else list.

- **First-person-singular is an error to fix, not a voice.** `[PROPOSED]` — The
  `/fillings-and-crowns` page reads *"I try viewing each patient… contact me
  today"* while every other page uses "we," and the practice has three dentists.
  Rule 5 standardizes on "we." Flagged `[PROPOSED]` rather than `[CONSTRUCTION]`
  because it is *possible* (unconfirmed) that a single dentist wrote and owns
  that page deliberately; if so, the practice can say so and we keep it. Absent
  that, "we" is correct for a three-dentist office.

- **The practice states; patients gush (Rule 9).** `[PROPOSED]` — Reading the
  effusive-reviews / measured-copy split as an intentional division of labor
  (rather than coincidence) is an inference, but it is the cleanest way to keep
  warmth credible and honor the "no fabricated warmth" anti-pattern: borrow the
  superlatives from the people who actually said them.

## Rejected directions

- **"Warm / caring / gentle" as the personality words.** Rejected — true but
  generic; they could describe any family dentist and fail the specificity pass.
  "Named / Plain-spoken / Steadying" say something the next practice down the
  road could not copy.

- **Leaning on the template subheads as voice evidence** ("The Care You
  Deserve," "Helping You Get Healthy," "Taking Care of You"). Rejected as voice
  signal — products.md records that four pages reuse "Taking Care of You" and
  three reuse "Helping You Get Healthy," i.e. they are unchanged Wix theme copy,
  evidence of the *template*, not the practice. Not cited in any rule.

- **A pricing-transparency *tagline* or brand line.** Out of scope (YAGNI) —
  taglines and headlines are Task 10–12 work, drafted against these rules. This
  file names the messaging *responsibility*, not the words.

- **Correcting patients' typos in quotes** ("with with regards," "then there,"
  "the bestest"). Rejected hard — quotes are facts, not prose; products.md
  gotcha 12 forbids tidying them. Rule 10 draws the line so this never happens
  by reflex.

## Open register — decisions without a settled answer

Carry forward; do not let these silently become rules.

1. **Does the practice deliberately publish prices, or is it a Wix habit?**
   Answers V2 and decides how hard "Plain-spoken" and the pricing-transparency
   thesis can lean. One question to the practice settles it.
2. **Who wrote the first-person `/fillings-and-crowns` page, and do they want it
   kept?** Answers V7. If a single dentist owns it deliberately, Rule 5 gets an
   exception; otherwise it standardizes to "we."
3. **Is "Steadying" the right third word, or does the practice see itself
   differently** (e.g. "thorough," which Tiffany and the reviews also support)?
   The anxiety evidence is strong, but the practice may lead with competence.
4. **How much of the reviews may the pitch quote, and may reviewer first names
   be shown?** Products.md publishes them as captured, but reuse on a *new*
   marketing site is a consent question for the practice, not a copy call.
5. **Whether the practice wants the six price contradictions surfaced as a pitch
   argument** ("your facts drift across pages") or quietly fixed. A pitch
   decision, not a voice decision, but it shapes how boldly the transparency
   theme is played.
6. **Whether there is brand/voice material off the website** (a written mission,
   an existing tagline, staff-authored copy guidelines). Everything here is
   reverse-engineered from a live Wix site; one question could replace a lot of
   inference.

## Owner correction round — 2026-07-22 (post-build)

The first built pages passed the original QA gates but failed the owner's ear: the hero lede
("You'll know the price before you're in the chair, and you'll know your dentist by name"),
the About lede ("...before you sit down"), and "Here's what things cost" were all rejected as
AI-cadence marketing voice. Root cause: the voice family banned hype and fear but did not ban
*cadence patterns* or title-restating ledes, so copy optimized toward punchy parallel hooks.
Six new `[RATIFIED]` anti-patterns added to voice.md (Rev 2). Call-button repetition was also
rejected ("this isn't a personal injury law firm"): phone is now presented as plain
"Phone: ..." text, once in the nav plus footer/contact. CLAUDE.md hard rules updated to match.
